About the Foundation

Brief History

Custody Care Foundation (CCF) is a non-government organization that is committed to improving the lives of vulnerable Nigerians by supporting the welfare of internally displaced persons across the country owing to the Boko Haram insurgency and by extension any other humanitarian crises that may occur in the country. It is also committed to supporting the government and other relevant stakeholders in the provision of quality education to the teeming Nigerian children; providing quality healthcare to all Nigerians and supporting people living with disabilities among other vulnerable people.

The foundation was started in June 2016 under the aegis of Forum of Internally Displaced Persons in the Southwest by Alhaji Buba Babagana, an indigene of Pulka - one of the epicenters of the insurgency - to primarily provide succor to IDPs in the South Western states of Nigeria as many of them have flown their original homes due to the ravaging crises in the North East. Many of them are left uncatered for as the focus light is always on those in the IDP camps. Some of these affected people have been relocated and resettled back to their states with the assistance of some stakeholders.

However, the scope of the foundation was later on expanded to cover all affected areas of the country and other humanitarian crises. It was fully incorporated with the Corporate Affairs Commission in 2020.

The Custody Care Foundation is committed to enhancing the quality of life of Nigerians by supporting initiatives that improve the livelihoods of IDPs, PLWDs, access to quality healthcare and educational opportunities and other humanitarian crises.

To contribute to building a Nigeria where all citizens, irrespective of their social backgrounds, religion or ethnicity, have access to quality life and are given equal opportunities to realize their potentials to the fullest.

  • To promote the preservation of dignity, encourage self-reliance, support the livelihoods, and improve opportunities for those displaced as a result of insurgency or any other humanitarian crisis or disaster.
  • To provide quality education especially to the girl-child who have been neglected in IDP camps and also provide educational materials for the camps.
  • To promote women livelihood and encourage leadership roles for women in IDP camps.
  • To promote activities that prevent and mitigate against climate change.
  • To promote advocacy issues as it relates to the plights of Borno/Yobe indigenes in the IDP camps and in diaspora.
  • To work with other relevant stakeholders to prevent internal displacement and minimising its negative effects.
  • To raise national awareness of the problem of displacement and assist in advocating the rights of the internally displaced persons and other victims of humanitarian disasters.
  • To assist victims of displacements and other humanitarian disasters reintegrate back into their societies through the provision of material and immaterial supports such as decent accommodation, means of livelihood, clothing and food for sustenance.
  • To assist every other needy in the society - such as widows and orphans, people living with disabilities, and other such people - with the basic necessities of life.
The following are the core values of the Foundation:
  • Transparency
  • Accountability
  • Fairness and equity
  • Empathy
  • Welfare of IDPs
  • Education
  • Environment/Climate Change
  • Gender mainstreaming
  • Health
  • Human Rights Advocay
  • Welfare of PLWDs

The foundation collaborates with relevant stakeholders to achieve her aims and deepen her core values in carrying out her activities on the identified focus areas. Such partners include corporate bodies, government, other NGOs, philanthropic individuals among others.
